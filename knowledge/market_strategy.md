# Crypto Market Strategy

## Bear Market Strategy
Bear market characteristics: prices down 70-90% from all-time highs, low trading volume, negative sentiment, project failures. Duration: typically 12-24 months in crypto cycles. Strategy: reduce altcoin exposure aggressively, accumulate high-conviction Layer 1 assets (BTC, ETH) via DCA, hold 30-50% in stablecoins for opportunity buying, avoid leverage entirely, focus on protocols generating real revenue. Bear markets are when wealth is accumulated — not when it is displayed.

## Bull Market Strategy
Bull market indicators: BTC dominance falling as capital rotates to altcoins, increasing retail interest, NFT volume surging, meme coin season. Strategy: take profits into strength on a schedule (not emotion), use a tiered exit — sell 25% at 2x, 50% at 5x, hold remainder with trailing stop. Reinvest into stablecoins or BTC. Avoid FOMO chasing parabolic assets at the top. Recognize that altcoin peaks often precede BTC peak by 2-4 weeks.

## DCA (Dollar Cost Averaging)
DCA removes timing risk by purchasing fixed amounts at regular intervals regardless of price. Effective for volatile assets like BTC and ETH over 12-36 month time horizons. DCA into bear markets historically produces strong returns over 4-year cycles. Combine with lump-sum purchases at technical support levels for enhanced positioning. Avoid DCA into low-cap altcoins — most do not recover from bear markets.

## Portfolio Construction
Conservative portfolio (low risk): 60% BTC, 30% ETH, 10% stablecoins. Moderate portfolio: 40% BTC, 30% ETH, 20% large-cap altcoins (SOL, AVAX, LINK), 10% stablecoins. Aggressive portfolio: 25% BTC, 20% ETH, 40% mid-cap altcoins, 15% small-cap speculative. Never allocate more than 5% to any single small-cap project. Rebalance quarterly. Always maintain a stablecoin reserve for buying opportunities.

## Risk Management
Position sizing: never risk more than 1-2% of portfolio on a single trade. Stop losses: set at meaningful technical levels, not arbitrary percentages. Leverage: avoid above 3x, use only with strict stop losses and small position sizes. The crypto market can drop 20-30% in 24 hours — size accordingly. Liquidity risk: small-cap tokens can become illiquid during crashes, making exits impossible at quoted prices.

## On-Chain Metrics
Key signals: Bitcoin exchange inflows (selling pressure), stablecoin supply ratio (buying power), funding rates (leverage sentiment), open interest (speculative positioning), NVT ratio (network value vs transactions — valuation signal), MVRV Z-score (market value vs realized value — overbought/oversold signal), Puell Multiple (miner revenue — cycle timing). Glassnode, Nansen, and CryptoQuant are primary data sources.

## Altcoin Selection Framework
Evaluate: real revenue (fees generated vs token emissions), token unlock schedule (cliff unlocks suppress price), team track record and doxxing, competitive moat (is the protocol genuinely differentiated), TVL trend (growing or declining), audit history, community health. Avoid: tokens with high fully diluted valuation vs current market cap ratio, protocols with anonymous teams and no audits, projects launching during bear markets with inflated valuations.

## Stablecoin Yield Strategy
Stablecoin yield sources: Aave/Compound lending (3-8% APY, low risk), Curve/Convex liquidity provision (5-15% APY, smart contract risk), centralized platforms like Coinbase (4-5% APY, custodial risk), US Treasury backed yields via Ondo or Mountain Protocol (4-5% APY, regulatory risk). Avoid unsustainable yields above 20% — these rely on token inflation not real revenue and collapse when sentiment turns.
